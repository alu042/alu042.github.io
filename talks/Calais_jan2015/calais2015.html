Bells and differential equations

Computing pullback along flows of vector fields on manifolds naturally leads to noncommutative Bell polynomials.


\[\frac{d^n}{dt^n}\bigg|_{t=0} \Phi^*_{t,F_t} \psi = B_n(F_1, \dots, F_n)[\psi],\]

where $F_{n+1} = F^{(n)}(0)$.

Example:


\[ \begin{align} \frac{d^3}{dt^3}\bigg|_{t=0} \Phi_{t,F}^* \psi &= B_3(F_1, F_2, F_3)[\psi]\\ &= F_1^{3}[\psi] + (F_2F_1)[\psi] + 2(F_1F_2)[\psi] + F_3[\psi] \end{align} \]

A generalization of the Faà di Bruno formula.

Bell polynomials and differential equations

Gives us a way to rewrite the Lie series for the pullback



\[\begin{eqnarray*}\Phi^*_{t,F} \psi &=& \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{t^n}{n!}\bigg(\frac{d^n}{dt^n}\bigg|_{t=0} \Phi_{t,F}^* \psi\bigg)\\ &=& \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{t^n}{n!}\bigg(B_n(F_1, \cdots F_n)[\psi] \bigg)\end{eqnarray*}\]

Incidence Hopf algebras

Incidence Hopf algebras


Theorem (Schmitt).
If

  • $\mathcal{P}$ is an hereditary family of finite posets
  • $\sim$ reduced order-compatible relation

then the vector space freely generated by $\mathcal{P}/\sim$ is a Hopf algebra, called the incidence Hopf algebra associated to the posets $\mathcal{P}$.

Basic ingredients:

  • Posets and intervals of posets
  • order-compatible relations
  • coalgebra and algebra structure
  • antipode

Poset

A partially ordered set $(P, \leq)$

Interval

$[x,y] \subset P$ is the set $\{z \in P : x \leq z \leq y\}$

Set partitions under refinement order

Order-compatible relation

Equivalence relation $\sim$ that is order preserving: $\, P \sim Q$ iff exists bijection $\varphi:P \rightarrow Q$ such that


\[[0_P, x] \sim [0_Q,\varphi(x)], \quad [x,1_P] \sim [\varphi(x), 1_Q]\]

Coalgebra and algebra structure


\[\Delta(\overline{P}) = \sum_{x \in P} \overline{[0_P, x]} \otimes \overline{[x,1_P]} \]

The cartesian product \[ \overline{P} \times \overline{Q}\] can be given the poset structure: $(s,t) < (s', t')$ iff $s < s'$ and $t < t'$.

Example: Faà di Bruno Hopf algebras

Poset of partitions under refinement:

$S \leq T$ iff all blocks of $S$ in blocks of $T$.

Gives an IHA on intervals $[S,T]$, where $\sim$ is poset isomorphism.

This is the commutative Faá di Bruno Hopf algebra

\[\Delta(X_n) = \sum_{m=0}^n B_{n+1,k+1} (X_0, X_1, \dots) \otimes X_k.\]

More interesting: Noncommutative Faà di Bruno Hopf algebra

Noncommutative Faà di Bruno Hopf algebra

Family of intervals $[S,T]$ of partitions of a totally ordered set, partially ordered by refinement.

Now

\[[S,T] \sim [S',T'] \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad S\simeq S', T \simeq T',\]

where $\simeq$ is as follows:

Noncommutative Faà di Bruno Hopf algebra

Assume

  • S a partition of $\,\{a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n\} =: A$
  • T a partition of $\,\{b_1, b_2, \dots, b_n\}=: B$

Then \[S \simeq T\] iff there is a bijection

\[\varphi: \{a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n\} \rightarrow \{b_1,b_2,\dots, b_n\}\]

that bijectively sends any block of $S$ to a block of $T$ and

\[\max(S_1) < \max(S_2) \Leftrightarrow \max \varphi(S_1) < \max \varphi(S_2) \]

Noncommutative Faà di Bruno Hopf algebra

We have

\[\begin{align} [0,S] &\cong \prod_{W \mbox{ is a block of S}} SP(W)\\\\ [S,1] &\cong SP([n+1]/S) = \mbox{part. of set of blocks of S} \end{align} \]

Obtain the IHA

\[\Delta(\overline{P}) = \sum_{x \in P} \overline{[0, x]} \otimes \overline{[x,1]}\]

which becomes the noncommutative Faà di Bruno Hopf algebra $DFdB$

\[ \Delta(X_n) = \sum_{m=0}^n B_{n+1,k+1} (X_0, X_1, \dots) \otimes X_k. \]

A more general noncommutative IHA


$Q$ is the family of intervals $[S,T]$ of partitions as before. Now


\[[S,T] \sim [S',T'] \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad S\simeq S', T \simeq T',\]

where $\simeq$ if the unique increasing bijection $\tau: A \rightarrow B$ sends any block of $S$ to a block of $S'$.

Get a noncommutative Hopf algebra $H$

Obtain surjections:



\[H \rightarrow DFdB \rightarrow FdB\]

Antipodes in incidence Hopf algebras

Antipodes in incidence Hopf algebras based on so-called uniform families can be written as quasideterminants.

For a uniform family of posets, IHA is $\mathbb{K}\langle x_1, x_2, \dots\rangle$, $deg(x_n) = n$, where $x_n$ is the unique indecomposable type of rank $n$.

\[ S(x_4) = \left|\begin{array}{cccc} W_{4,3} & W_{4,2} & W_{4,1} & W_{4,0} \\ 1 & W_{3,2} & W_{3,1} & W_{3,0}\\ 0 & 1 & W_{2,1} & W_{2,0} \\ 0 & 0& 1& W_{1,0} \end{array}\right|_{1,4} \]

  • The $W_{n,k}$ are so-called rank polynomials, generalizing Bell polynomials
  • Extends a result by Schmitt from commutative to noncommutative
