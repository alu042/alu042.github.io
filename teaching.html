MSc projects

I'm currently supervising four MSc candidates (more details about the project will be added):

  • Sindre Eik de Lange and Stian Heilund (2017—2019). Autonomous mobile therapeutic robot. Using advanced computer vision techniques, and the Robot Operating System, the project aims at developing a robotic physiotherapautphysical therapist for patient rehabilitation.
  • Sathiesh Kumar Kaliyugarasan (2017—2019). Transfer learning in medical imaging. A study of how to best use transfer learning when training deep neural networks for biomedical image analysis.
  • Sean Meling Murray (2017—2018). An Exploratory Data Analysis of Uncertainty Estimation in Bayesian Neural Networks. Sean is developing and exploring techniques for obtaining robust uncertainty estimates for deep neural networks.

Feel free to contact me if you're interested in doing a MSc project in machine learning.

BSc projects

    Jon Einar Haraldsvik, Stian Gudvangen Gjerløw, Didrik Fanuelsen Tranvåg (2015). Tryg Maintenance App – A cross-platform application using Appcelerator Studio Cloud Services and Arrow DB. The students developed a cross-platform mobile application with features meant as support for maintenance related issues for Trtg Forsikring. The project was awarded "best bachelor project" at the department in 2016, and the students went on to start Appivate AS.

Feel free to contact me if you're interested in doing a BSc project.

I'm currently developing two new courses:

  • DAT158: Machine learning and advanced algorithms. A practical, project-based, hands-on exploration of the fundamentals of machine learning, focusing on solving real-world problems.
  • ELMED219, an elective course in the University of Bergen medical school. The course will be offered to both medical students and engineering students and encourage collaborations between these disciplines, motivated by e.g. "The convergence revolution".

In addition to lecturing in these courses I'm creating supporting e-learning material: MAT106x, BMED360x, ELMED219x, NordBiomed, financed by the Erasmus+ KA2 strategic partnerships MedIm and HVL. To deliver these and other courses I've set up the e-learning platform AkademiX.
